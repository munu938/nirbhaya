What is ADHD?

Attention Deficit Hyperactivity Disorder (ADHD) is a neurodevelopmental disorder characterized by persistent patterns of inattention, hyperactivity, and impulsivity that interfere with functioning or development. It affects both children and adults, impacting their ability to focus, control behavior, and manage daily activities.

ADHD is one of the most common mental health conditions affecting children and often continues into adulthood. While everyone experiences periods of inattention or hyperactivity, people with ADHD experience these symptoms more severely and frequently, significantly impacting their quality of life.

Risk Factors

Genetics

Family history of ADHD increases risk. Studies show 74-91% heritability rate.

Prenatal Factors

Maternal smoking, alcohol use, or drug use during pregnancy.

Brain Injury

Traumatic brain injury or infections affecting the brain.

Premature Birth

Low birth weight or premature delivery.

Environmental Toxins

Exposure to lead or other environmental toxins at a young age.

Gender

Males are more likely to be diagnosed, though females may be underdiagnosed.

Symptoms

Inattention

  • Difficulty sustaining attention
  • Doesn't seem to listen when spoken to
  • Fails to follow through on instructions
  • Difficulty organizing tasks
  • Avoids tasks requiring sustained effort
  • Often loses things
  • Easily distracted
  • Forgetful in daily activities

Hyperactivity

  • Fidgets with hands or feet
  • Often leaves seat inappropriately
  • Runs or climbs excessively
  • Unable to play quietly
  • Often "on the go"
  • Talks excessively
  • Restlessness
  • Difficulty engaging in leisure activities

Impulsivity

  • Blurts out answers
  • Difficulty waiting turn
  • Interrupts or intrudes on others
  • Makes decisions without thinking
  • Difficulty controlling emotions
  • Acts without considering consequences
  • Impatience
  • Risk-taking behaviors

Treatment Approaches:

Medication: Stimulants and non-stimulants can help manage symptoms

Behavioral Therapy: CBT and behavior modification techniques

Lifestyle Changes: Regular exercise, healthy diet, adequate sleep

Educational Support: IEPs, 504 plans, and classroom accommodations

Support Groups: Connect with others who understand the challenges

Important: This information is for educational purposes only. If you suspect you or someone you know has ADHD, please consult with a qualified healthcare professional for proper diagnosis and treatment.
