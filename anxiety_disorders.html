Types of Anxiety Disorders

üå™Ô∏è Generalized Anxiety Disorder (GAD)

Persistent, excessive worry about various aspects of life including work, health, family, and everyday situations. Symptoms last for months and interfere with daily activities.

üò∞ Panic Disorder

Recurrent, unexpected panic attacks with intense fear and physical symptoms like racing heart, sweating, and feeling of impending doom. Often leads to fear of future attacks.

üò± Specific Phobias

Intense, irrational fear of specific objects or situations such as heights, animals, flying, or medical procedures. The fear is disproportionate to actual danger.

üë• Social Anxiety Disorder

Intense fear of social situations due to concerns about being judged, embarrassed, or humiliated. Can significantly impact work, school, and relationships.

üè† Agoraphobia

Fear of being in situations where escape might be difficult or help unavailable during a panic attack. Often involves avoiding crowded places, public transportation, or being alone outside.

üîÑ Obsessive-Compulsive Disorder (OCD)

Unwanted, intrusive thoughts (obsessions) and repetitive behaviors or mental acts (compulsions) performed to reduce anxiety or prevent feared outcomes.

üß† Mental Symptoms

  • Excessive worry or fear
  • Racing thoughts
  • Difficulty concentrating
  • Feeling restless or on edge
  • Anticipating the worst
  • Irritability
  • Memory problems

üíì Physical Symptoms

  • Rapid heartbeat
  • Shortness of breath
  • Sweating or trembling
  • Muscle tension
  • Nausea or stomach upset
  • Dizziness or lightheadedness
  • Fatigue or insomnia

üë§ Behavioral Symptoms

  • Avoiding certain situations
  • Procrastination
  • Seeking constant reassurance
  • Difficulty making decisions
  • Restlessness or fidgeting
  • Social withdrawal
  • Compulsive behaviors

üåô Sleep & Daily Life

  • Trouble falling asleep
  • Frequent waking at night
  • Difficulty with work/school
  • Relationship problems
  • Avoiding responsibilities
  • Loss of interest in activities
  • Substance use as coping

Professional Treatment Options

üß† Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT)

Evidence-based therapy that helps identify and change negative thought patterns and behaviors that contribute to anxiety.

  • Most effective for anxiety disorders
  • Teaches practical coping skills
  • Usually 12-20 sessions
  • Focuses on present challenges

üå± Exposure Therapy

Gradual, controlled exposure to feared situations or objects to reduce avoidance and anxiety responses over time.

  • Highly effective for phobias
  • Builds confidence gradually
  • Can be done virtually or in-person
  • Long-lasting results

üíä Medication Management

Anti-anxiety medications and antidepressants can help manage symptoms while learning coping strategies in therapy.

  • SSRIs and SNRIs commonly used
  • Can provide quick symptom relief
  • Best combined with therapy
  • Regular monitoring needed

üßò Mindfulness-Based Approaches

Incorporates meditation, breathing techniques, and present-moment awareness to reduce anxiety and stress.

  • Reduces overall stress levels
  • Improves emotional regulation
  • Can be practiced anywhere
  • Complements other treatments

Self-Help Tools & Techniques

These techniques can help manage anxiety symptoms between therapy sessions or as preventive measures.

üå¨Ô∏è Deep Breathing Exercises

Try the 4-7-8 technique: Inhale for 4 counts, hold for 7, exhale for 8. Repeat 4 times to activate your body's relaxation response.

üèÉ Progressive Muscle Relaxation

Tense and release each muscle group for 5 seconds, starting from your toes and working up to your head. Helps release physical tension.

üìù Thought Challenging

Question anxious thoughts: Is this realistic? What evidence do I have? What would I tell a friend? Replace with balanced thoughts.

üî¢ 5-4-3-2-1 Grounding

Name 5 things you see, 4 you can touch, 3 you hear, 2 you smell, 1 you taste. Brings focus back to the present moment.

üì± Anxiety Apps

Headspace, Calm, DARE, and Anxiety Coach offer guided meditations, breathing exercises, and anxiety management tools.

üí™ Regular Exercise

Just 20-30 minutes of moderate exercise can reduce anxiety symptoms. Walking, yoga, swimming, or dancing all help.
